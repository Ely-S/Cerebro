DETAILED VERIFICATION CALCULATIONS

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. LOGIC AND POWER DOMAIN  --  R-ELEC-01, R-ELEC-02
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Arduino Nano 33 IoT: 3.3 V I/O logic throughout.
ADS1115 module: VDD connected to Arduino 3.3 V rail per schematic.
I2C lines (A4/SDA, A5/SCL): 3.3 V compatible on both devices.
LED drivers: powered from external 5 V via TIP31C transistors.
Ground: single common GND required and specified in assembly notes.
Spec explicitly warns against connecting ADS1115 VDD to 5 V. Risk identified;
design addresses it correctly in schematic.

Verdict R-ELEC-01: PASS.
Verdict R-ELEC-02: PASS.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. LED DRIVER CURRENT AND SATURATION MARGIN  --  R-DRV-01
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Assumptions:
  V_gpio   = 3.3 V
  V_BE     = 0.7 V
  R_base   = 470 ohm
  V_CE_sat = 0.2 V (conservative at ~100 mA)

Base current:
  I_B = (3.3 - 0.7) / 470 = 5.53 mA

LED currents (external 5 V, channel-specific series resistors):
  730 nm (V_F ~ 2.1 V): I_LED = (5.0 - 0.2 - 2.1) / 68 = 39.7 mA
  850 nm (V_F = 1.60 V per SFH4253B datasheet at 100mA, tp=20ms):
    I_LED = (5.0 - 0.2 - 1.60) / 68 = 47.1 mA
  [ISSUE-003 corrected: prior calculation used V_F = 1.5V (unspecified estimate).
   SFH4253B datasheet (PDF v1.2) gives V_F typ = 1.60V at 100mA explicitly.
   Corrected current is 47.1mA, within the new 40-50mA band.]

Both values meet the updated V&V bands:
  730 nm: 35-50 mA  (calc: 39.7 mA -- PASS)
  850 nm: 40-50 mA  (calc: 47.1 mA -- PASS)

Required forced-beta for saturation:
  730 nm: h_FE_required = 39.7 / 5.53 = 7.2
  850 nm: h_FE_required = 47.1 / 5.53 = 8.5

TIP31C datasheet: h_FE minimum ~25 at 1 A, typically > 50 at 100 mA. Nominal
margin is adequate.

Conditional note: A conservative forced-beta design (h_FE_forced = 10) would
require I_B ~ 9-10 mA, implying R_base ~ 260 ohm. The updated 470 ohm value
gives h_FE_forced ~16-17, materially improving saturation margin while staying
within practical MCU-drive limits. Bench Test 3.2 is required for formal
closure across device and temperature variation.

Verdict R-DRV-01: PASS (conditional). Bench Test 3.2 required.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. RESISTOR THERMAL DISSIPATION  --  R-THERM-01
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Continuous (100% duty) worst case:
  P_730 = (0.0397)^2 * 68 = 0.107 W
  P_850 = (0.0471)^2 * 68 = 0.151 W

Both are below the 0.5 W continuous resistor rating.

Under 4-state TDM at 1/4 duty cycle per LED:
  P_avg_730 = 0.107 / 4 = 0.027 W
  P_avg_850 = 0.151 / 4 = 0.038 W

Verdict R-THERM-01: PASS.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. STAGE 1 TIA TRANSIMPEDANCE  --  R-AFE-01
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Schematic specifies 100 kohm metal-film 1% feedback resistor with 4.7 pF
stability capacitor across it.

Nominal transimpedance = 100 kohm.
V&V plan acceptance: 100 kohm +/- 5% (95 kohm to 105 kohm).

Paper review confirms correct component values are specified. Actual measured
gain requires bench current-injection test (V&V Test 3.3) for formal closure.

Verdict R-AFE-01: PASS (conditional). Bench Test 3.3 required.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. STAGE 2 VOLTAGE GAIN  --  R-AFE-02
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Non-inverting amplifier configuration:
  Gain = 1 + R_f / R_g = 1 + 47k / 1k = 48 V/V (nominal)

V&V plan acceptance: 48 V/V +/- 5% (45.6 V/V to 50.4 V/V).
Total equivalent transimpedance: 100 kohm * 48 = 4.8 Mohm.

Bench Test 3.4 required for formal closure.

Verdict R-AFE-02: PASS (conditional). Bench Test 3.4 required.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. CLIPPING HEADROOM AND STABILITY  --  R-AFE-03
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Maximum photocurrent before ADC clipping (PGA = +/-2.048 V):
  I_max = 2.048 / 4.8e6 = 426.7 nA

Expected tissue photocurrent: ~10-100 nA (BPW34 responsivity ~0.6 A/W at 850 nm;
received optical power in the nW range after 30 mm tissue path). This is well
within the linear operating range with ~4x-40x margin to full-scale.

Stability pole analysis:
  Stage 1 TIA bandwidth: f_c1 = 1 / (2*pi * 100k * 4.7pF) = 338.6 kHz
  Stage 2 closed-loop BW: GBW / gain = 10 MHz / 48 = 208.3 kHz

Physiological content is < 10 Hz. With 4-state TDM at 64 SPS, per-channel update
rate is 16 Hz. Both are far below the stage cutoff frequencies. The 4.7 pF compensation
capacitor also limits noise gain at the BPW34 input capacitance (~70 pF).

Conditional note: Phase margin depends on real PCB trace parasitics and layout.
Oscillation cannot be ruled out from paper review alone. Bench Tests 3.3 and 3.4
must confirm no sustained oscillation or ringing.

Verdict R-AFE-03: PASS (conditional). Bench Tests 3.3, 3.4, 4.1, 4.4 required.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. TDM TIMING ANALYSIS  --  R-TIME-01  [PASS (conditional)]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

At ADS1115 64 SPS:
  T_conversion = 1 / 64 = 15.625 ms per single conversion

Minimum 4-state cycle (AmbientA -> Red -> AmbientB -> IR), one conversion/state:
  T_cycle_min = 4 * 15.625 = 62.5 ms
  Per-channel update rate = 1 / 0.0625 = 16.0 Hz

Important interpretation: 62.5 ms is an ideal lower bound, not a guaranteed
runtime value. Actual cycle time can increase due to ADC settling behavior,
I2C/config transaction latency (if rewritten per state), LED switching delays,
and loop overhead. Formal closure requires bench timing capture (e.g., GPIO
strobe plus scope/logic analyzer) during V&V Test 3.7.

Spec Rev 3.2 now states a theoretical minimum 4-state cycle of 62.5 ms at
64 SPS and requires measured runtime confirmation in V&V Test 3.7.

Downstream impact: The spec states a 10 Hz output rate. This is achievable
because serial output cadence is decoupled from internal TDM cycle timing.
The per-channel effective sample rate (16 Hz) is now documented consistently
and is used in the EMA time-constant analysis (see Section 9).

Verdict R-TIME-01: PASS (conditional). Bench Test 3.7 required.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. EMA FILTER DEFINITION AND TIME CONSTANT  --  R-FW-01  [OPEN]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

EMA formula: y[n] = alpha * x[n] + (1 - alpha) * y[n-1],  alpha = 0.025

Spec Rev 3.2 explicitly fixes the convention to:
  N_eff = 1/alpha = 40 samples.

Effective averaging time at per-channel 16 Hz update rate:
  40 / 16 = 2.5 seconds of averaging.

This resolves the prior ambiguity and aligns spec text with V&V Test 3.8.
Remaining work is implementation verification in firmware logs/bench testing.

Verdict R-FW-01: OPEN pending Tests 3.8 and 4.5.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
10. PROBE OPTICAL ISOLATION  --  R-OPT-01  [OPEN]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Spec assembly step 4 identifies direct LED-to-photodiode light coupling as "the
#1 failure point" and mandates an opaque rubber washer stack to restrict the wide 130-degree beam angle of the 730nm Star Board, plus opaque foam/tape isolation and a dark-space
verification step. This is the correct mitigation approach.

Paper review cannot assess isolation quality. V&V plan requires:
  Test 2.6: Mechanical and optical build inspection (ruler, bright backlight).
  Test 3.9: Ambient-subtracted dark test with serial logging; readings must
             remain near zero with no large fixed offset.

Verdict R-OPT-01: OPEN. Assembly and bench dark test required.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
11. OPTICAL SAFETY  --  R-SAFE-01  [PASS (conditional) -- bench closure required]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[ISSUE-002 -- 2026-03-01: RESOLVED. R-SAFE-01 verdict changed to PASS (conditional).
 The original estimate for 850nm peak optical power (40mW) was accurate for ~45-50mA,
 but the resistor was incorrectly specified at 36 Ohms. Changing the resistor to 68 Ohms
 brings the current to ~47.1mA, where the peak power is ~40mW.
 At 4-state TDM duty cycle, the time-averaged power is ~10mW, meeting the criterion.]

LED peak optical powers (revised, from SFH4253B datasheet + Star Board data):
  730 nm: ~35-40 mW peak (linear estimate: 310mW@350mA * 40mA/350mA ~ 35mW;
           40mW is a conservative upper bound. Actual value requires bench measurement.)
  850 nm: ~36-40 mW peak (interpolated from datasheet at ~47mA)

At 4-state TDM duty cycle (each LED on during one of four ADC states):
  730 nm average: ~35-40 mW / 4 = ~8.8-10.0 mW  (marginal; bench measurement mandatory)
  850 nm average: ~36-40 mW / 4 = ~9.0-10.0 mW   (near limit; bench measurement mandatory)

Interpretation note: The spec Rev 3.2 criterion is time-averaged emitted power
per channel < 10mW under final timing/drive settings.

Additional note: IEC 62471 risk-group status for SFH4253B is not explicitly
validated by a direct citation in this report and is therefore treated as
unverified pending a primary source reference.
This is separate from the project's self-imposed <10mW time-averaged criterion.
Whether to revise the project criterion to IEC 62471 basis is a design decision
requiring instructor/lead approval per V&V plan gate rules.

Formal closure still requires bench optical power measurement in V&V Test 3.10.
The current paper estimate with 68 ohm drive predicts compliance with the
project criterion, but the margin is tight and must be confirmed by measurement.

Verdict R-SAFE-01: PASS (conditional). Bench Test 3.10 required.

12. PRECISION CLAIM  --  R-PREC-01  [OPEN -- OPEN-002]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Claim: +/-2% relative precision at 95% confidence (spec Section 3).

Required evidence for closure:
  - Defined stationary test condition (probe fixed, no motion, stable ambient).
  - Sample count sufficient for the chosen confidence interval method
    (minimum N = 30; proper power analysis recommended).
  - Documented estimator: e.g., sample standard deviation used to compute a
    two-sided 95% CI on individual measurements or on the mean.
  - Acceptance test: CI half-width must be <= 2%.

None of this evidence exists at the paper stage. The SNR calculation cited in
spec Section 12 ("SNR ~38:1 calculated") is not a substitute for a formal
statistical validation protocol. This item requires bench execution of V&V
plan Test 4.6.

Note: R-FW-01 remains OPEN until firmware-level EMA behavior is verified.

Verdict R-PREC-01: OPEN. OPEN-002. Bench Test 4.6 required.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SUPPORTING NOTE: WAVELENGTH SUITABILITY (not a V&V plan requirement)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

730 nm falls on the deoxygenated-hemoglobin-dominant side of the hemoglobin
absorption crossover (~800 nm isosbestic point). 850 nm falls on the
oxygenated-hemoglobin-dominant side. This standard pairing provides differential
sensitivity to cerebral oxygenation changes and is consistent with published NIRS
instruments and the references cited in spec Section 14. No V&V requirement is
assigned to this; it is recorded here as supporting rationale.

CA-003  --  EMA Definition Standardization                       [APPLIED]

  Implemented:
    - Convention fixed to N_eff = 1/alpha = 40 in spec and V&V plan.
  Remaining closure:
    - Firmware behavior and log-level verification in Tests 3.8 and 4.5.

CA-004  --  Transistor Drive Margin                              [APPLIED]

  Implemented:
    - Base resistor changed from 1 kohm to 470 ohm in design documentation.
  Rationale:
    h_FE_forced = 10 (conservative minimum for reliable saturation)
    I_B_required = I_LED_max / h_FE_forced = 100 mA / 10 = 10 mA
    R_base_required = (3.3 - 0.7) / 0.010 = 260 ohm
  Remaining closure:
    - Verify V_CE saturation margin and channel currents in Test 3.2.

CA-005  --  Power Rail Bulk Decoupling                           [APPLIED]

  Implemented:
    - Added 47-100uF bulk capacitor at 5V rail entry.
    - Added 10uF bulk capacitor near analog 3.3V rail.
  Rationale:
    - Reduce rail ripple coupling from LED switching transients into analog path.
  Remaining closure:
    - Confirm rail behavior during switching and no brownout/reset in Tests 3.1/3.2.
